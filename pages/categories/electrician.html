Frequently Asked Questions

What constitutes an electrical emergency? +

Electrical emergencies include: power outages affecting your property only, sparking outlets or switches, burning smells from electrical panels, exposed wires, electrical shocks, and smoke from electrical devices. For emergencies, we offer 24/7 service with rapid response times.

Do I need a permit for electrical work? +

Most significant electrical work requires permits including panel upgrades, new circuits, whole house rewiring, and additions. As licensed electricians, we handle all necessary permits and coordinate inspections with local authorities. Simple repairs and replacements typically don't require permits.

How often should I have my electrical system inspected? +

For homes under 40 years old, every 10 years is recommended. For older homes (40+ years), every 3-5 years is advisable. Commercial properties should be inspected annually. Always get an inspection before purchasing a property or if you're experiencing frequent electrical issues.

What are signs I need an electrical panel upgrade? +

Signs include: frequent circuit breaker tripping, using multiple power strips, flickering lights when appliances turn on, burning smell from the panel, rust or corrosion on the panel, adding major appliances (EV charger, hot tub, AC unit), or having a panel with fuses instead of breakers.
